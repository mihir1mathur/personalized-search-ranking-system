================================================================
WEEK 1: FAILURE CASES -- WHERE KEYWORD RETRIEVAL STRUGGLES
Project 3: Personalized Search Ranking System
================================================================

This file looks at real queries where our keyword methods (TF-IDF
and BM25) did a poor job. Keyword methods can only match the exact
words that appear in the text. When the shopper's words differ from
the product's words -- even if they MEAN the same thing -- keyword
search misses the relevant product.

For each case we show:
  1. Query
  2. Known relevant product(s) (the ground-truth answer)
  3. TF-IDF Top-5 results
  4. BM25 Top-5 results
  5. Why keyword matching failed
  6. What semantic (meaning-based) retrieval may understand

(rel = known relevance_score; 3 = highly relevant ... 0 = irrelevant.
A retrieved product with no judgment for this query shows rel=0.)


================================================================
CASE 1
================================================================
1. QUERY: holiday pjs

2. KNOWN RELEVANT PRODUCT(S):
   - B07NSPNCCS (rel=2)  Simple Joys by Carter's Baby 2-Pack
                         Christmas Fleece Footed pajamas

3. TF-IDF TOP-5:
   1. B074XHCTVC  Holiday Engagement                       (rel=0)
   2. B076VMC1HT  Chapstick Holiday Collection 2017        (rel=0)
   3. B08932YG81  Airplane Pajamas for Boys Girls PJs       (rel=0)
   4. B086XJKNQW  Pajamagram Teen Girls Pajamas            (rel=0)
   5. B06XTR9DMK  Pajamagram Flannel PJs For Women         (rel=0)

4. BM25 TOP-5:
   1. B086XJKNQW  Pajamagram Teen Girls Pajamas            (rel=0)
   2. B01NCL1X7I  PajamaGram Womens Pajamas Soft Cotton    (rel=0)
   3. B06XTR9DMK  Pajamagram Flannel PJs For Women         (rel=0)
   4. B015IXRH04  Ekouaer Women's Pj Set                   (rel=0)
   5. B08932YG81  Airplane Pajamas for Boys Girls PJs       (rel=0)

5. WHY KEYWORD MATCHING FAILED:
   The relevant product never uses the word "pjs" -- it says
   "pajamas" -- and it never uses "holiday", it says "Christmas".
   TF-IDF latched onto the literal word "holiday" and returned a
   movie ("Holiday Engagement") and lip balm ("Holiday
   Collection"). Neither method connected "pjs" to "pajamas" or
   "holiday" to "Christmas", so the truly relevant item was missed.

6. WHAT SEMANTIC RETRIEVAL MAY UNDERSTAND:
   An embedding model learns that "pjs" and "pajamas" mean the same
   thing, and that "holiday" is closely related to "Christmas". It
   would therefore rank the Christmas pajamas product near the top.

CASE 2
================================================================
1. QUERY: portable bidet

2. KNOWN RELEVANT PRODUCT(S):
   - B01N2MCLG5 (rel=1)  Bidet Handheld Water Sprayer PORTABLE
                         Rechargeable Battery

3. TF-IDF TOP-5:
   1. B08VJBSB47  Hot and Cold Bidet Mixing Valve Kit       (rel=0)
   2. B0815B8VZR  Brondell Swash Ecoseat Bidet Toilet Seat  (rel=0)
   3. B00A0RHSJO  LUXE Bidet Neo 120 (toilet attachment)    (rel=0)
   4. B074MMWZYQ  Brondell Swash SE400 Seat                 (rel=0)
   5. B08NWDJB8Z  Zenvida Bidet For Toilet                  (rel=0)

4. BM25 TOP-5:
   1. B08VJBSB47  Hot and Cold Bidet Mixing Valve Kit       (rel=0)
   2. B08NWDJB8Z  Zenvida Bidet For Toilet                  (rel=0)
   3. B00A0RHSJO  LUXE Bidet Neo 120 (toilet attachment)    (rel=0)
   4. B0815B8VZR  Brondell Swash Ecoseat Bidet Toilet Seat  (rel=0)
   5. B07KX9J9DQ  Brondell Thinline SimpleSpa (toilet)      (rel=0)

5. WHY KEYWORD MATCHING FAILED:
   The word "bidet" is everywhere in this category, so both methods
   flooded the top results with bidet TOILET SEATS and valves. They
   over-weighted the common word "bidet" and effectively ignored the
   shopper's real intent word, "portable". The one product that is
   actually a portable, handheld bidet got buried below the fixed
   toilet-seat bidets.

6. WHAT SEMANTIC RETRIEVAL MAY UNDERSTAND:
   A meaning-based model understands that "portable" implies a
   small, handheld, battery-powered device -- not a bolted-on toilet
   seat. It can push the handheld portable bidet up and the fixed
   toilet seats down, even though both contain the word "bidet".

CASE 3
================================================================
1. QUERY: horror bedding sets queen

2. KNOWN RELEVANT PRODUCT(S):
   - B07ZGG597L (rel=3)  CHASOEA Duvet Cover Set, Decorative
                         HORROR Clown Face (queen bedding)

3. TF-IDF TOP-5:
   1. B089K97L3C  ENCOFT Dragon 3D Comforter Bedding Sets   (rel=0)
   2. B07X43NKP6  Carnival of Souls Halloween Movie Poster  (rel=0)
   3. B07481NDKZ  Happiness: A Fickle Queen (a book)        (rel=0)
   4. B07YDLNGC7  CLOTHKNOW White Plaid Comforter Queen     (rel=0)
   5. B085RQRS6R  Freak Of Horror Coloring Book             (rel=0)

4. BM25 TOP-5:
   1. B089K97L3C  ENCOFT Dragon 3D Comforter Bedding Sets   (rel=0)
   2. B07R4659ZY  Bedsure Queen Comforter Set               (rel=0)
   3. B074TFZF6V  HIGHBUY Full Bedding Sets Queen           (rel=0)
   4. B07YDLNGC7  CLOTHKNOW White Plaid Comforter Queen     (rel=0)
   5. B081GXYS6P  FADFAY Floral Duvet Covers Queen          (rel=0)

5. WHY KEYWORD MATCHING FAILED:
   The query mixes a THEME ("horror") with a product type ("bedding
   sets queen"). TF-IDF matched "horror" to a horror movie poster
   and a horror coloring book, and matched "queen" to a book titled
   "...A Fickle Queen". BM25 ignored the theme and returned generic
   queen bedding (dragon, plaid, floral). Neither could find bedding
   that is specifically horror-THEMED, because that requires
   understanding the theme, not just matching the word.

6. WHAT SEMANTIC RETRIEVAL MAY UNDERSTAND:
   A semantic model understands that "horror bedding" means spooky /
   scary-themed bedding (clowns, skulls, Halloween motifs) AND that
   it must still be a queen-size bedding set. It can combine theme +
   product type and surface the horror clown duvet cover.

CASE 4
================================================================
1. QUERY: tankless water heater

2. KNOWN RELEVANT PRODUCT(S):
   - B0084CPEDE (rel=3)  Rinnai V65EN Tankless Hot Water Heater,
                         6.5 GPM, Natural Gas

3. TF-IDF TOP-5:
   1. B07VWQC7R8  Tankless Water Heater Electric ECOTOUCH   (rel=0)
   2. B00TUC3ETI  Reliance 30 gallon Electric (has a TANK)  (rel=0)
   3. B00IX0V57Q  Titan N-120 Electronic Tankless           (rel=0)
   4. B07JVH69C1  Libra Tankless Isolation Valve (a part)   (rel=0)
   5. B07RS9TLVN  1300W Submersible Portable heater         (rel=0)

4. BM25 TOP-5:
   1. B07VWQC7R8  Tankless Water Heater Electric ECOTOUCH   (rel=0)
   2. B00IX0V57Q  Titan N-120 Electronic Tankless           (rel=0)
   3. B07JVH69C1  Libra Tankless Isolation Valve (a part)   (rel=0)
   4. B0084CPDFS  Rheem Tankless Natural Gas                (rel=0)
   5. B00DQYHVJ0  EZ 202 Tankless Water Heater - Propane    (rel=0)

5. WHY KEYWORD MATCHING FAILED:
   This is the closest call -- keyword search DOES return many
   tankless heaters because the exact words match. But it cannot
   tell the important differences apart: it returned a 30-gallon
   TANK heater (the opposite of tankless), an isolation VALVE (just
   a part, not a heater), and electric units, while the specific
   relevant Rinnai gas unit did not make the top 5. Keyword scoring
   counts shared words; it does not understand "tankless vs tank",
   "heater vs valve/part", or "gas vs electric".

6. WHAT SEMANTIC RETRIEVAL MAY UNDERSTAND:
   A meaning-based model can distinguish a true tankless gas heater
   from a tank heater, an accessory/part, or an electric unit, and
   match the shopper's actual product intent more precisely.

CASE 5
================================================================
1. QUERY: apple phone charger cord and plug

2. KNOWN RELEVANT PRODUCT(S):
   - B014LFT8H0 (rel=1)  Apple Lightning to USB CABLE (2 m)
   - B01F9RGSFE (rel=1)  Amazon Basics Nylon Braided Lightning CABLE
   - B07VX8VF2L (rel=1)  2-Pack Phone Charger USB Cable for iPhone

3. TF-IDF TOP-5:
   1. B087FRRN8Y  Phone Charger Cable                       (rel=0)
   2. B083J9SL6T  LETSCOM Replacement Charging Cable        (rel=0)
   3. B07X1C1QRJ  2Pack Phone Charger to USB Cable          (rel=0)
   4. B0009TCDZ2  Zoeller Sump Pump (!)                     (rel=0)
   5. B079PT75R8  Furbo Dog Camera Charger Cord             (rel=0)

4. BM25 TOP-5:
   1. B07MQ8WWF6  Dual Port USB Wall Charger / Plug         (rel=0)
   2. B082X6Q8T7  WAITIEE Wireless Charger 3 in 1           (rel=0)
   3. B082X6N8PD  WAITIEE Wireless Charger 3 in 1           (rel=0)
   4. B07T4MC66M  USB C Fast Charger for iPad Pro           (rel=0)
   5. B077CZM19N  BEACOO Charging Stand for iWatch          (rel=0)

5. WHY KEYWORD MATCHING FAILED:
   The shopper said "cord", but the relevant Apple products say
   "cable". The shopper said "apple phone", but the products say
   "Lightning" and "iPhone". Because the exact words differ, keyword
   search wandered off: TF-IDF even returned a SUMP PUMP (it matched
   the words "and"/"plug"/"cord"), and BM25 returned wall chargers,
   wireless chargers, and an iWatch stand. None of the actual Apple
   lightning cables reached the top 5.

6. WHAT SEMANTIC RETRIEVAL MAY UNDERSTAND:
   A semantic model knows "cord" = "cable", "apple phone" = "iPhone",
   and that an iPhone charging cord is a "Lightning to USB cable".
   It would connect the shopper's everyday words to the product's
   technical words and rank the real Apple cables near the top.


================================================================
SUMMARY
================================================================
Across all five cases the pattern is the same: TF-IDF and BM25 are
strong when the shopper uses the SAME words as the product, but they
break down on synonyms (cord/cable), abbreviations (pjs/pajamas),
themes (horror), and intent words (portable, tankless). They match
words, not meaning. This is exactly the gap that Week 2's semantic /
embedding-based retrieval is designed to close.
